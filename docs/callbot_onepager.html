CallBot: automated call-testing rig

A PC-controlled dialer plus a small pro-audio bridge turns panel call-testing from a fragile, manual chore into a fully scripted, unattended process — using any phone, with no phone modification.

Working on the bench today · one ~$210 hardware request completes it

The problem

Panel call-testing needs a phone that answers the panel's emergency calls and behaves like a real person on the line. We drove that phone with crude ADB key-presses — unreliable, and unable to send DTMF tones or control the call audio at all. That left far-end disconnect codes, simulated conversation, and call recording untestable in any automated way.

What works today — CallBot app

CallBot installs as the phone's dialer and is driven entirely from the PC (with a normal dialer screen for manual use). Reliable call control, real DTMF tones, a live on-screen event log, and a scripting interface that plugs into our test suite. It already delivered a result we could not get before:

161 / 161 unattended overnight panel calls passed — and it proved DTMF “9” reliably ends the panel's call (58/58), a detail needed for the open field bug.

Why this is the right solution

  • Phone-agnostic — works with our current phone and any future one, any carrier. Bluetooth hands-free is universal; nothing is tied to a specific model.
  • No rooting, no phone modification — nothing that voids warranty, weakens security, or breaks on an OS update.
  • Everything runs from the PC — control and audio are centralized, so an entire call scenario is scriptable, repeatable and unattended.
  • Off-the-shelf, proven hardware — a podcast interface, not a hack.
